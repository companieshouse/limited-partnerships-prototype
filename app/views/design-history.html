Week ending Fri 24 Jan 2025 {{ govukTag({ text: "V6" }) }}

  • Added a heading to the text we have on the Start pages around users being able to adjust their devices, on the advice of our Senior Accessibility Specialist. (Example - Registration start page). Intent is this text/heading will be on all CH Start pages (user research has consistently proven two things: people who make minor adjustments such as increased text size do not seem themselves as users with accessibility needs, and many people do not realise these adjustments can be made).
  • Main focus this week was addresses. Have improved the functionality of the postcode look-up pattern so it's closer to what live will be - users can now opt to enter a manual address and opt to 'adjust' the address the postcode look-up suggests (for example, to add a “C/O” note). On advice of the business we've also re-introduced the Jurisdiction page, because of complexities around rules of where the Registered Office Address and Principal Place Of Business Address must be in relation to the type of company. This Jurisdiction page is dynamic based on the type of partnership being registered (Scottish or Eng/Wales/NI)

Week ending Fri 17 Jan 2025 {{ govukTag({ text: "V6" }) }}

  • Main focus was revisiting the 'Transition' journey in the prototype. Checking it still works correctly (it shares pages with the Registration journey). Had to 'remove' some pages (including IDV, Terms of Partnership, SIC codes, Principal Place of Business) and make a few other minor adjustments based on the latest requirements. The aim is to make as much of the design and content across both Transition and Registration as generic as possible so CH does not need to design, build and maintain two versions of each screen.
  • Some users are still not understanding the name endings pattern on Registration, trying to click the grey suffix box. And we know from conversations with teams on the Register your company service name ending screen, a small minority are still typing in a name ending. So, I designed 3 alternatives: a suffix box which does not look like a button, a select (drop-down) for users to choose a name ending, and swapping the order; name ending first, name second. We will get user feedback on these and monitor the live service when it goes in to see if a change is needed.
  • Held a design crit of the Registration service prototype with the interaction design team - got some great feedback, but it was mostly suggestions that are currently not in scope because of project deadlines.
  • Alongside the other UCD people working on the project, we showcased the UCD work we have been doing at a User-Centred Design Show and Tell session.

Week ending Fri 10 Jan 2025 {{ govukTag({ text: "V6" }) }}

  • Added a proposed Confirm your chosen partnership name screen. We know from the Register your company service that a small number of users are still entering a name ending even though we add this for them, MY COMPANY LIMITED LIMITED. We had a very useful session with the internal support team this week and they confirmed this - users who make the mistake need to file a change of name form. This proposed screen will not go in for the first release - we hope that playing back the chosen name (and ending) at the top of screens throughout the rest of the registration journey will be enough for users to realise when a mistake has been made (name is also featured on Check your answers of course).
  • Alongside the other UCD people working on the project, we showcased the Registration prototype to the internal support team - got some good, useful feedback.

Week ending Fri 27 Dec 2024 {{ govukTag({ text: "V6" }) }}

  • Designed two alternative patterns to gather 'capital contribution' data - the first pattern we built based on requirements only catered for a GBP value, but user research showed that users may want to enter a different currency or a non-monetary value.
  • Removed identity verification screens (for example, Identity verification for general partners) from the Registration journey, as updated requirements meant these were no longer in scope.
  • A general improvement of the realism of the prototype, while we had time - much more of the users entered data is shown later on in the prototype.
  • Prototyped the 'Upload a document to Companies House' service where users would supply PSC info for Scottish LPs/PFLPs. Completing this action as part of the main LP service is currently not in scope.
  • What is the principal place of business? - adjusted the 'different' address pattern. Originally the proposal was for a postcode look-up, but because post-Registration this can be an overseas address, it is more efficient to use a 'standard' address pattern and simply make the County field dynamic (hidden at Registration because addresses need to be in the UK, shown post-Registration because the address can be overseas)
  • Added in the Welsh language toggle - unlikely to be functional in the prototype.
  • Updated service banner on payment screens, for example Payment review. These screens are separate to the main service and so cannot yet use the new service name pattern. Note, these screens should include the service name in the black GOV header, but don't at the time of writing.
  • Added in the option to submit a second nationality on the Add a general partner (person) and Add a limited partner (person) screens, based on new requirements.
