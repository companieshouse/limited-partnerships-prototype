File a limited partnership's transition update

Use this service to file a transition update for an existing limited partnership.

All limited partnerships must file a transition update by XX DATE. This is due to new measures introduced by the Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023.

Who can use this service

The update must be filed by a Companies House authorised agent. This is also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP).

What a Companies House authorised agent is and how to find one

Authorised agents are businesses that are registered with a UK Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body. For example, accountants, solicitors or company formation agents.

They must register as an authorised agent with Companies House to be able to file on behalf of clients.

To find an authorised agent, you should contact agents to check:

  • if they are registered as an authorised agent with Companies House - this is also known as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)
  • if they can offer the services you need
  • how much they charge for their services

Before you start

All general partners need to verify their identity for Companies House. If a general partner is a legal entity, a registered officer for the entity will need to verify.

What is a registered officer?
A registered officer must be a director of the entity, or someone whose roles and responsibilities correspond to that of a company director.

If you’re filing an update for a Scottish limited partnership, people with significant control will also need to verify their identity.

When they've verified, we'll give them a Companies House personal code, which they will need to share with you. You will need to provide their personal code as part of the update.

What you'll need

You'll need to tell us information that was given when the partnership was registered and new details that are now required by law. This will include details about the:

  • limited partership
  • general partners
  • limited partners
  • people with significant control, if you’re providing an update about a Scottish limited partnership (SLP)

If you need to check what information was given when the partnership was registered, you can view the partnership’s filing history on the Companies House register.

To use the service, you'll need to:

  • sign in, or create sign in details
  • give the limited partnership number
  • pay £XX using a credit or debit card
